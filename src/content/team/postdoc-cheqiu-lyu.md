Dr. Cheqiu Lyu is a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Astronomy at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC). He obtained his Ph.D. in Astrophysics from the Kavli Institute for Astronomy and Astrophysics (KIAA) at Peking University (PKU) in 2024, after earning his B.S. in Astronomy from Beijing Normal University (BNU) in 2019.

His research focuses on galaxy formation and evolution, with a primary interest in galaxy assembly, chemical evolution, and the galaxy-halo connection. By leveraging observational data from SDSS, DESI, and JWST, alongside numerical simulations such as IllustrisTNG and L-GALAXIES, he investigates the physical mechanisms driving galaxy growth, star formation quenching, and gas flows across cosmic time.

Beyond his research, Cheqiu is also an active science popularizer, book translator, teenager astronomy lecturer, and amateur astronomical observations instructor.
