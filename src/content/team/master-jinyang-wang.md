Jinyang Wang is a Master's student in astrophysics at the University of Science and Technology of China, advised by Prof. Enci Wang.

His research uses massive spectroscopic and imaging datasets from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) and the Hubble Space Telescope (HST) to study the formation, evolution, and assembly modes of high-redshift galaxies. His recent work focuses on the enhancement and suppression of internal star formation across different redshifts and stellar masses, as well as transitions in galaxy assembly modes during the cosmic noon epoch.
