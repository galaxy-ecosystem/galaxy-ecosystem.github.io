Prof. Enci Wang was born in 1990 in Lixin County, Bozhou, Anhui Province. He received his B.S. in Astronomy from the University of Science and Technology of China in 2011 and his Ph.D. in Astrophysics from Shanghai Astronomical Observatory, Chinese Academy of Sciences, in 2016. From June 2016 to June 2018, he conducted postdoctoral research at the University of Science and Technology of China, followed by a second postdoctoral appointment at ETH Zurich from July 2018 to April 2023. Since May 2023, he has served as a Specially Appointed Professor in the Department of Astronomy at the University of Science and Technology of China.

His research focuses on galaxy formation and evolution. His major scientific contributions include using the time-varying properties of galaxies to explain the scatter in star formation rates and metallicities, opening a time-domain perspective on galaxy studies; developing a theoretical framework for the coupled evolution of star formation, metal enrichment, and cold gas content, revealing the origins of star formation rate variability across different galactic scales; and establishing a leaky accretion-disk model for galactic gas disks that quantitatively explains radial metallicity gradients and proposes a physical mechanism for exponential disk formation.

As of April 2023, he had published more than 30 SCI-indexed papers, including 17 as first author or corresponding author in journals such as ApJ and MNRAS, and has served as a referee for leading international journals. Selected contribution papers are available through his [ADS library](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/user/libraries/IlyTUs8oRS-82uJyXE9qlw).
