Haoran Yu is a Ph.D. student in astrophysics at the University of Science and Technology of China, advised by Prof. Enci Wang. He received his B.Sc. in Astrophysics from USTC in 2023.

His research focuses on baryon distribution in the circumgalactic and intergalactic medium, the cosmic web, and the baryon cycle traced by metal absorption lines. He works with large spectroscopic and imaging datasets from DESI, JWST, and HST, including stacking analyses of background QSO spectra and galaxy spectra.

Haoran's recent work includes first-author studies on stellar feedback and baryon deficiency in low-mass galaxies, and on constraining AGN host-galaxy properties through image and spectral energy distribution decomposition. He is also a co-first author on JWST measurements of Mg II-traced cool gas outflows toward cosmic dawn.
