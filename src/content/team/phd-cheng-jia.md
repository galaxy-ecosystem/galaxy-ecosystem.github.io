Cheng Jia is a Ph.D. student in astrophysics at the University of Science and Technology of China, advised by Prof. Enci Wang. He received his B.S. in Astrophysics from USTC in 2023 and his M.S. in Astrophysics from USTC in 2025.

His research focuses on high-redshift galaxies, including their star-formation histories, size evolution, and chemical enrichment. He uses JWST imaging and photometry, galaxy morphology measurements, and spectral energy distribution fitting to study galaxy evolution.

Cheng's recent first-author work uses JADES observations to investigate the wavelength-dependent size variation of star-forming galaxies and the gas-phase metallicity of galaxies at redshifts 1 < z < 7.
